/dev-thoughts

I’ve always been more of a backend and cloud person — APIs, logic, pipelines, data flow — that’s my comfort zone. So, building something this visual was a bit of a challenge. This portfolio started as a simple idea to showcase my work, but along the way I realized "how" I present my work matters just as much as "what" I build.

What you see here took a lot more time and late nights than I’d planned. I spent hours reading UI/UX blogs, pestering front-end friends for design advice, and yes — borderline harassing AI chatbots to fix layout bugs that refused to go away. There were moments of pure frustration and then those quiet 3 AM wins when something finally clicked. And honestly? I loved every bit of it.

I tried to give this site the same feel as my daily dev setup — a clean, Arch-inspired terminal aesthetic with a backend developer’s personality baked in. It’s far from perfect — there are still rough edges and small things I want to polish — but I built it the way I’d want to use it myself: simple, readable, and focused.

This isn’t the final version. I’ll keep improving it — just like I try to improve my craft, one commit at a time. For now, this is my small corner on the internet — where code meets character.

#Thanks for making it this far.
It genuinely means a lot. If you have any feedback — big or small — I’d love to hear it. Every little suggestion helps me build this better. ❤️
